🔎 Reference Integrity Checker

Paste a bibliography — one reference per line — and check each entry against open scholarly databases: does it exist, do the metadata match, and has it been retracted. Everything runs in your browser; no reference ever leaves your device except as a query to public APIs.

How to read this. “Not verifiable” does not mean “fabricated.” It means the reference was not found in Crossref or OpenAlex — which also happens for legitimate books, reports, and articles in regional or non-indexed journals. Treat it as a prompt to check by hand, not a verdict. This web version queries two databases; the research pipeline adds a third (Semantic Scholar) and human review.
Sources: Crossref and OpenAlex (free, no key). Open-source and part of an ongoing scientometric study on citation integrity — code & method on GitHub. Verdicts are advisory, never definitive.
